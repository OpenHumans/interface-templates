What does this do?

This site allows you to connect data from external data source to your Open Humans account. After logging in with your Open Humans account you can upload data/link data from external data source.

How does it work?

You can log in into this site with your Open Humans account. This allows us to deposit data in your Open Humans account.

After this you can upload data from external data source to put them in your Open Humans account.

After this you can connect your external data source account to this site. This enables us to access your external data source data. We will automatically ask external data source for new data in regular intervals. These data will be written into an archive in your Open Humans account.

Your data is private by default. Open Humans gives you the ability to share data with projects you select, but only if and when you choose to do so.

When you connect data from external data source, this site will access the following data and store it in your Open Humans account.

  1. x

  3. z
  4. etc.

The site also stores the following data when you connect accounts from external data source and Open Humans.

  1. an anonymous identifier for your Open Humans account
  2. authentication credentials that allow us to read & write data from external data source to Open Humans
  3. (authentication credentials that allow us to read your data from external data source)

Finally, this site's hoster Heroku stores and gives us access to the IP addresses of visitors, and pages visited on this site. These logs may be stored for up to a week.

You can always delete your account and all associated data from the Dashboard when being logged in. This includes the Open Humans and external data source authentication credentials, i.e. we won't be able to access your Open Humans & external data source accounts any longer.

Removing the authorization on Open Humans does not automatically delete your Oura archive on Open Humans; if you want this to occur, you should delete your account here before withdrawing from this project in the Open Humans site.
